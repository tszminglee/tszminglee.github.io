About

Professional

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Linguistics at University of Southern California. I received my MA in Linguistics from Kyoto University and my BA in Japanese Studies and Linguistics from The Chinese University of Hong Kong.

My research focuses on questions concerning the syntactic structure of natural languages and its mapping onto meanings and sounds, and seeks to further our understanding on these issues through the lens of East Asian languages. I am particularly interested in movement theories, issues relating to compositionality, linearization, and modality. (see Projects for more details).

Full CV

I initiated the Forum on Cantonese Linguistics in 2018, and host an annual forum in Cantonese linguistics since then.

I run a reading group with other grad students on Chinese syntax and semantics, discussing recent papers in the area. Let me know if you are interested!
